# Blockchain Technologies for Improving Booking Processes in the Hotel Business

This repository contains the working materials for a bachelor's thesis in the `Hotel Business` field. The research focuses on how blockchain technologies can be applied to improve hotel booking processes, reduce operational frictions in digital distribution, and strengthen the economic efficiency of booking management.

The thesis examines the hotel market context, compares existing blockchain approaches relevant to hospitality, and proposes a practical implementation model for a mid-scale urban hotel. The project combines academic writing, market-based analytical inputs, and spreadsheet models used to justify the proposed solution.

## Thesis Focus

The study is centered on the following topic:

`Application of Blockchain Technologies to Improve Booking Processes in the Hotel Business`

Main research directions:

- analysis of centralized online booking models and their limitations in hospitality;
- review of blockchain platforms and architectures applicable to hotel operations;
- design of a hybrid blockchain implementation model for hotel booking workflows;
- economic justification of implementation using open market data and spreadsheet calculations;
- formulation of practical recommendations for phased pilot deployment.

## Academic Context

The thesis is prepared within the higher education direction:

- `43.03.03 Hotel Business`
- profile: `Hotel Operations`

The practical model is built around a 4-star Moscow hotel with 100 rooms and evaluates the feasibility of introducing a blockchain-based booking status verification layer on top of an existing hotel digital infrastructure.

## Methodological Base

The work combines:

- academic literature review;
- comparative analysis of blockchain platforms;
- hospitality process modeling;
- market-based assumptions from open industry sources;
- economic efficiency calculations, including ROI, payback period, and NPV.

## Notes

This repository is an academic research project, not a production hotel software system. The blockchain model presented in the thesis is conceptual and analytical in nature and is intended to support research conclusions and implementation recommendations for the hospitality sector.
